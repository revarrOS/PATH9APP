# Reflection and Self-Assessment

Regular reflection helps you learn from experience, adjust your approach, and maintain clarity about what matters most.

## Why Reflection Matters

Without reflection, we repeat the same patterns without learning. Reflection transforms experience into wisdom.

## Simple Reflection Questions

### Daily Reflection (5 minutes)
- What went well today?
- What challenged me?
- What did I learn?
- What will I do differently tomorrow?

### Weekly Reflection (15-20 minutes)
- What progress did I make this week?
- What obstacles did I encounter?
- What am I grateful for?
- What needs my attention next week?

### Monthly Reflection (30 minutes)
- Am I still aligned with my goals?
- What patterns am I noticing?
- What do I want to continue?
- What do I want to change?

## Journaling as Reflection

Writing clarifies thinking. You don't need to write pages—even a few sentences can provide valuable insight.

## The Gap vs. The Gain

When reflecting, focus on progress (the gain) rather than the distance remaining (the gap). Measure backward from where you started, not forward to where you want to be.

## Questions for Stuck Moments

When feeling stuck or uncertain:
- What's really bothering me about this situation?
- What assumptions am I making?
- What would I tell a friend in this situation?
- What's one small step I could take?

## Building Reflection into Your Routine

Reflection is most effective when it's regular and structured. Choose a consistent time—morning coffee, evening wind-down, or Sunday afternoon.

## Reflection is Not Rumination

Healthy reflection moves you forward. Rumination keeps you stuck in circles. If reflection makes you feel worse without insights, it's time to shift to action or seek support.
