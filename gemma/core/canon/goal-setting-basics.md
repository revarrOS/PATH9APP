# Goal Setting Basics

Effective goal setting is a foundational skill for personal development. Goals provide direction, motivation, and a framework for measuring progress.

## SMART Goals Framework

The SMART framework helps create well-defined goals:

- **Specific**: Clearly define what you want to achieve
- **Measurable**: Include criteria to track progress
- **Achievable**: Set realistic goals within your capabilities
- **Relevant**: Align goals with your values and priorities
- **Time-bound**: Set a clear timeframe for completion

## Starting with Small Goals

When beginning your journey, start with small, manageable goals. This builds confidence and creates momentum. A small win today is better than an abandoned ambitious goal tomorrow.

## Examples of Well-Formed Goals

- "Exercise for 20 minutes, 3 times per week for the next month"
- "Read one personal development book per quarter"
- "Practice mindfulness meditation for 5 minutes each morning"

## Common Goal-Setting Mistakes

- Setting too many goals at once
- Making goals too vague or abstract
- Not breaking large goals into smaller steps
- Ignoring the "why" behind your goals
- Not reviewing and adjusting goals regularly

## Next Steps

Once you have a clear goal, break it down into actionable steps. Identify the first small action you can take today.
