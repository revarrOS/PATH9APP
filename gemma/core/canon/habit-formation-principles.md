# Habit Formation Principles

Habits are the compound interest of self-improvement. Understanding how habits form helps you intentionally create positive behaviors and reduce negative ones.

## The Habit Loop

Every habit follows a three-part loop:

1. **Cue**: A trigger that initiates the behavior
2. **Routine**: The behavior itself
3. **Reward**: The benefit you gain from the behavior

## Making Habits Stick

### Start Small (The 2-Minute Rule)

When building a new habit, start with a version that takes two minutes or less. Want to build a reading habit? Start with one page per day. Want to exercise? Start with putting on workout clothes.

### Stack Habits

Link new habits to existing ones. After you pour your morning coffee (existing habit), do one minute of stretching (new habit). This leverages existing neural pathways.

### Design Your Environment

Make good habits obvious and easy. Want to drink more water? Place water bottles in visible locations. Want to reduce phone usage? Keep your phone in another room while working.

## Breaking Bad Habits

To break a bad habit, focus on:
- Identifying the cue that triggers it
- Finding a healthier routine that provides a similar reward
- Making the bad habit harder to do (increase friction)

## Patience with the Process

Research suggests habits take 21 to 66 days to form, with an average of 66 days. Progress isn't linear. Missing one day doesn't ruin your progress—just return to the habit the next day.

## Tracking Progress

Track your habits visually. Each day you complete the habit, mark it on a calendar. The chain of marks becomes motivation not to break the streak.
